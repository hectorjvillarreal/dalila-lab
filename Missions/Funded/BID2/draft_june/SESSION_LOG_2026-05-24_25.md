## 1. What the experiment is

A **comparative-steady-state** experiment for the IDB seminar (2026-06-05). The
gender extension of the DraftApril OLG model is solved at three calibrations
of the demographic-fiscal closure, all sharing the same preferences,
technology, and (provisional) sex-asymmetry stubs:

| Closure | What clears PAYG | What clears general budget |
|---|---|---|
| **RUN 1** — 2020 baseline (joint closure) | endogenous τ^p | endogenous B (residual) |
| **C1** — 2050 demographics, joint closure | endogenous τ^p | endogenous B (residual) |
| **C2** — 2050 demographics, τ^p pinned | benefits scale to balance PAYG | endogenous B (residual) |

C1 isolates the *pension-contribution channel* of aging: how much must τ^p rise
when policy lets it absorb the demographic shock. C2 asks: *what if τ^p doesn't
adjust* — how do retirees and households respond?

A fourth closure (**C3 — debt absorbs**, τ^p pinned with general government
debt funding the pension shortfall) was explored and produced a paper-motivation
finding (see §3); it does *not* yield a finite SS at the 2050 calibration and
is not in the seminar table.

Spec: `CC_instrucciones_aging_steady_state.md`. Stationary equilibria only;
no transition path; Mexico only.

### What the numbers say

1. **Aging nearly doubles the pension contribution rate** when policy lets
   τ^p clear PAYG (14.5% → 26.4%). This is the standard headline.
2. **B/Y falls from 28.3% to 22.9% under C1.** Because τ^p absorbs the
   pension shock, the general budget actually has more room — capital
   accumulates, capital income tax revenue rises. Counterintuitive but
   model-consistent.
3. **C2 is welfare-superior to C1 for high-skill types of both sexes.**
   When contributions don't adjust, households save more (precautionary)
   for their smaller future pension. K rises another 32% beyond C1. The
   richer household stock more than offsets the smaller pension flow for
   high-skill cohorts.
4. **C2 is welfare-inferior to C1 for low-skill women.** Low-skill women
   have less ability to self-insure via savings; they're hurt more by the
   benefit cut than helped by higher capital income.
5. **The sex gap closes under aging.** RUN 1: men have welfare ~5%
   above women at each skill. C1: nearly zero gap. The higher τ^p eats
   more of the productive-male income wedge.

The qualitative gender story (a closing sex gap as aging hits) is robust;
the magnitudes are stub-dependent (e^F = 0.85·e^M and a 25% mortality
uplift for women — provisional, to be replaced with WPP-derived sex-specific
primitives in September).

## 3. Paper-motivation finding: C2 with debt-absorbs has no finite SS

The spec's "B absorbs" wording in C2 has two readings:

1. Pension benefits stay at `κ·w·L/N^W` (the calibrated formula). The
   resulting PAYG deficit flows into general government, so B funds both
   the regular general budget AND the pension shortfall.
2. Pension benefits scale to balance PAYG at the pinned τ^p
   (`pen = τ^p·w·L/N^R`). Aging incidence falls on retirees as a benefit
   cut. B unchanged from the spec's default closure.

Interpretation **2** is what we ship as the C2 column above. Interpretation
**1** was tested and **admits no finite stationary equilibrium**:

```
iter   K       L       r(5y)    DIFF/Y
1     12.00   10.00   0.163    -0.79
7     42.25   21.88   0.029    -0.084
8     54.70   22.57  -0.022    -0.112   ← r drops below n_p
9    557.29   23.28  -0.274    -1.363   ← K explodes
13    54.16   27.10   0.021    -0.011
14    79.08   26.25  -0.065    -0.113   ← oscillating
```

**Mechanism.** Pension deficit pushes general primary deep into deficit →
B becomes deeply negative → K_target = A_dom − B blows up → r falls →
r^n − n_p crosses zero from above → the SS debt equation
`B = primary/(r^n − n_p)` becomes ill-conditioned → K_target ricochets.

**Plot:** `results/c2_interp1_crash_panel.png` — three panels, the middle
one shows r crossing n_p at iter 8 (exactly when K explodes). This is the
single most economically articulate plot from this experiment.

**Economic interpretation.** At Mexico's 2050 calibration, the 2020
contribution rate is too low by a margin that *cannot be financed via
general debt at any finite SS*. The pension shortfall is large enough to
push the model into dynamic inefficiency (r^n < n_p), where the standard
transversality argument fixing B at a finite level breaks down.

**Implication.** "Do nothing on either contribution OR benefit margins" is
not just expensive — it's mathematically infeasible. *Something* must
adjust: τ^p (C1), benefits (C2), or general taxation (C3). The seminar
narrative can be reframed from "aging is costly" to "the menu of feasible
policies under projected demographics is narrower than commonly assumed."

Full mechanism + math: `C2_interp1_no_finite_SS.md`.

## 5. Code changes to `ge_model_gender.jl` (relative to Diego's source)

Deliberate spec deviations, all needed for the experiment to converge. Each
documented inline in the file and in the demographic_experiment README.

1. **Threading.** `Threads.@threads` on the inner cell loop in
   `solve_household_for_type` over a flat `k` index; per-thread
   `linint_Grow` scratch buffers; `Threads.Atomic{Int}` for `n_refined`.
   Ported from `draft_260519/ge_model.jl` commits 054687c + f3110c1.
   Yields ~3.7× speedup per type-solve on 16 cores (limited by the
   serial 4-types outer loop).
2. **GE-loop robustness.** `damp_ge` lowered from 0.50 to 0.30; positivity
   floor `max(K_upd, 0.5)`, `max(L_upd, 0.5)`. Without these, gender_gap
   primitives cause iter-3 K to go negative and crash the firm FOC.
3. **`n_p` and `ψ_base` from `const` to TYPED GLOBAL.** Julia 1.x silently
   allows `const` redefinition but compiled methods keep the inlined
   value, so the driver's `set_demographics!` override didn't propagate.
   Typed globals avoid inlining; `setglobal!` works as intended.

The driver also uses `Base.invokelatest(solve_ge!)` to defeat method-dispatch
world-age caching across `update_pension_taxes!` redefinitions.

Both fixes are saved as memories:
[[feedback-julia-const-redef-inlining]] and the threading port commit
provenance is in `f3110c1` and `054687c`.

## 6. Quality / precision caveats

- **C2 welfare** is bit-precise (independently re-derived from a dedicated
  standalone driver, matches to 4 decimals).
- **RUN 1 and C1 welfare** come from a focused recovery driver that runs
  `solve_household` at known (K, L) equilibria with τ^p / pen consolidated
  via a 3-iter inner loop. This converges to within 9e-6 in τp but
  truncates before a few sub-corner cells reach their full-GE optimum.
  - RUN 1's Euler max log10 = −0.61 (a handful of cells with near-corner
    `a'`; mass-weighted residual is good)
  - C1's `|A_dom − K − B|/K` = 13% (the inner loop locked τ^p/pen but
    didn't fully reconstruct the prior equilibrium's aggregate
    distribution)
  - The welfare values are *economically sensible* (ordering and
    magnitudes track) but **not bit-precise**. For paper-grade welfare
    numbers, a full GE re-run of RUN 1 and C1 would tighten these
    (~5 h cost).
- **Gender-gap Euler diagnostic** (`diagnose_gender_gap_euler.jl`)
  confirmed that the spec's worry about gender_gap-mode Euler residuals
  was a stale concern — current source has mass-weighted interior
  max log10 = −9.14. Welfare-by-sex is reportable. [[project-bid2-gender-gap-euler-ok]]
