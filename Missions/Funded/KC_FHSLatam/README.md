## What this mission is

This mission governs the research, institutional design, and knowledge production work associated with the **Comunidad Latinoamericana del Conocimiento sobre Financiamiento de la Salud** — a regional knowledge community anchored in the **Tríada** (Tec de Monterrey, Universidad de los Andes, Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile).

The community was seeded by a **$100,000 USD donation from Roche** (November 2025 agreement), with Tec de Monterrey as operational lead and Héctor as signing investigator. The first operational horizon is **January–December 2026**, with a five-year strategic horizon.

This is not a standard BDH research mission. It combines:
- **Academic production** (three Scopus-track papers in 2026)
- **Institutional design** (governance, sustainability strategy)
- **Community building** (regional network, inaugural seminar, public dissemination)

---

## Intellectual identity

The community's center of gravity is the question:

> **How do Latin American health systems remain fiscally sustainable and institutionally credible through the demographic and epidemiological transition?**

This framing is deliberately open — it invites multiple analytical traditions — but it is not infinitely elastic. It excludes purely clinical, purely access-equity, and purely pharmaceutical framings. Its disciplinary center is at the intersection of **public finance, health economics, demographic analysis, and political economy**.

BDH's comparative advantage within this space is the **macrofiscal and intertemporal angle**: the region's limited fiscal space, rising debt, and accelerating epidemiological transition create a structural fiscal problem that most health economists are not equipped to analyze rigorously. This angle is BDH's contribution, not an imposed identity for the community.

## Open items and risks

- **Roche independence risk:** Pharmaceutical funder of a health financing knowledge community creates a credibility exposure. Governance design must include explicit independence protections. Sustainability strategy must reduce Roche dependency by year two.
- **PI bandwidth:** This mission has the potential to become Héctor's most publicly visible work. Scope management is a standing concern.
- **Community identity drift:** Regional knowledge communities tend to drift toward convening over producing. The founding papers are the primary instrument for establishing and maintaining analytical identity.
- **Paper coherence:** Three independently conceived papers weaken the founding statement. Coordination on complementary angles is a priority for the inaugural conversations with Ramiro and Pablo.
- **Sustainability funding:** The $40,000 institutional design budget must produce a credible multi-year funding strategy. Grant seeking (Karina Sánchez) needs early activation.
